History

The International Conference on Computational Sciences and Engineering (ICCSE), formerly being held by Institute of Computational Sciences and Technology (ICST, Hochiminh city, Vietnam) emerged as a prominent platform for researchers, practitioners, and students to exchange knowledge in computational science, engineering, and interdisciplinary applications.

The fifth edition, ICCSE-5, marked a key milestone in this series. ICCSE-5 brought together international and local experts to advance the frontiers of computational methods in science, technology, and society. The conference provided a venue for rigorous academic exchange, interdisciplinary dialogue, and regional collaboration, particularly emphasizing the role of computational modeling in addressing real-world challenges.

Founded in the early 2010s, the ICSE played a pioneering role in Vietnam’s computational science landscape. Through the ICCSE conference series, the institute nurtured scientific talent and supported the growth of computational research both nationally and in the broader Southeast Asian region.

However, the global disruption caused by the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 brought unforeseen challenges to academic exchange. In its aftermath, the ICST concluded its mission, having significantly contributed to the development of Vietnam’s scientific infrastructure and computational capacity. ICCSE, held before that period, became the final major scientific event under the ICST’s organization, symbolizing the culmination of a decade of dedicated scientific engagement.

While the ICST has since concluded its institutional operations, the legacy of ICCSE—and particularly the ICCSE-1 to ICCSE-4—continues to inspire collaborations and initiatives across computational science communities in the region.
